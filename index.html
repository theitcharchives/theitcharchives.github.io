My name is Greer W. Hollow

I've chosen my name myself, piece by piece. You can call me Hollow.

I chose Hollow in middle school. It well represents how I felt back then and to this day–always wandering, looking for something, persistently clueless. The world is full of wonders, or so I have been told.

I chose Greer in my fresh twenties during a long car ride, after I realised gender was none of my business. It has the added bonus of carrying some continuity from my given name in how I find it difficult to pronounce.

The W. is a personal one. There's many reasons for that.

What about "itch", then

I'm a plotter, but only when I feel like it.
I wait for that tingle in my fingertips that compels me to make something.
It is not a completely pleasant sensation–there's truly no better way to describe it than to call it an itch.
Everything I create comes from that itch. Sometimes I manage to scrounge up some force of will, too, but simply put I don't like it.

No pictures, please

I'm staying incognito. This is how I'd like to look like.

This isn't a blog, I use Tumblr for that.
